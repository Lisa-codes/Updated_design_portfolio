Bowling Queue System

As the Lead UI/UX Designer, I developed an internal bowling queue system to efficiently manage high-traffic days at the Village Market mall's bowling alley. This system was created to streamline the booking process, making it easier for staff to handle large client volumes, particularly during peak times such as public holidays and weekends. Key features include "notified/pending" status bars for tracking ticket information and clickable lane cards for editing lane occupancy. The design adheres to WCAG guidelines, ensuring consistency and accessibility. The project was completed within a week, from June 7th to June 14th, and was designed to be used by administrators only, who were the target end users. The final product proved to be seamless, user-friendly, accessible, and easy to use.

Client: Bowling Alley Staff - Village Market

Sector: Administration

Project Duration: June 7th - June 14th, 2024 (1 Week)

Role: Lead UI/UX Designer

Design Process

Due to the project’s tight timeline, a rapid prototyping approach was utilized to quickly iterate and refine the design based on feedback. Initial wireframes were created to map out the user flow, focusing on ease of use and quick navigation for the bowling alley staff. High-fidelity mockups followed, which were crucial in visualizing the final product before development.

The design process was centered around the end users—the administrators—ensuring that the interface was intuitive and aligned with their daily tasks. The emphasis was on accessibility, following WCAG guidelines to make the system usable for all staff members, regardless of any potential limitations.

User Flow

The user flow for the bowling queue system starts when an administrator logs into the system. The admin can view available lanes, check the status of active games, and manage new bookings. Metrics & Performance Improvement

The bowling queue system had a significant impact on the operational performance of the bowling alley. Below are the key metrics showcasing the improvement after official handing over to the developers and successful implementation of the system:

  • Queue handling time reduced by 40% during peak hours.
  • Operational efficiency increased by 25%, leading to more seamless lane assignments.
  • Customer wait times decreased by 30%, leading to improved customer satisfaction.
  • Administrative errors reduced by 50%, streamlining overall operations.

Future Considerations

Moving forward, a key improvement I would implement is adding a user journey from the sign-up process for administrators. Given the time-sensitive nature of the project, the system was initially designed with the assumption that all users were already logged in as administrators. However, introducing a sign-up and login feature would enhance the system’s security and provide a more structured user experience.

Additionally, expanding the system's functionality to include detailed user profiles for each administrator could further streamline operations by tracking individual performance and usage statistics. This would provide valuable insights for management while improving the overall efficiency of the bowling alley operations.
